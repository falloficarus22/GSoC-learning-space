# Motivation

## Who I am

I am a self-taught developer currently studying AI deeply, with some basic background in robotics as well. Professionally, I am a forex trader, and at this stage of my career I am actively using open source as a way to improve both my technical skills and my understanding of real-world software development.

I have been working with Python for around three years, while my open-source experience is still relatively recent at about five months. In that time, I have contributed not only to Mesa-related repositories but also to projects like OpenCV and Kornia. I still see myself as someone learning by doing, and that is one of the reasons Mesa appealed to me.

## Why Mesa

I first came across Mesa while looking through the organizations participating in last year's Google Summer of Code. Compared to some much larger projects, Mesa felt like a place where I could realistically learn, contribute, and grow while still working on meaningful problems.

I am new to agent-based modeling, which is also part of why Mesa interested me. I did not want to contribute to a project only at the code level without understanding how users actually think with it. Mesa seems like a good place to learn both the modeling side and the framework side at the same time.

## What I want to learn

My main goal is to better understand agent-based modeling itself: how to think in terms of agents, interactions, groups, emergence, and higher-level behavior that arises from simple local rules. Since I am still new to ABM, I want to build that foundation by creating models and seeing where the framework feels intuitive and where it creates friction.

Within Mesa, my strongest interest is in `meta_agents`. I find the idea of representing higher-level structures out of lower-level agents very compelling, because it connects modeling, abstraction, and framework design in a way that feels both practical and conceptually rich. I am especially interested in how such abstractions should behave from a modeller's point of view, not just how they are implemented internally.

Through this process, I want to develop stronger skills in reading unfamiliar codebases, reasoning about APIs, designing features from a user perspective, writing cleaner contributions, and collaborating more effectively through reviews and discussion.

## Where I want to go

I want to make contributions that genuinely improve my skills while also being useful to the project. So far I have already contributed across different parts of the Mesa ecosystem, including `mesa-examples`, and I want to keep building on that experience in a more focused way.

For me, GSoC would be a major learning experience in software development as a whole. I want it to push me to improve not only as a programmer, but also as a contributor who can understand a problem well, communicate clearly, and work with maintainers and other contributors in a productive way. It would also be an important milestone for my long-term growth and future opportunities.

I would like to stay involved with Mesa after GSoC as well. My goal is not just to participate for one summer, but to grow into someone who can continue contributing meaningfully over time.
